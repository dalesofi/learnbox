# LEARNBOX — Product Foundation Document (MVP)

## Product Overview

LEARNBOX is a subscription-based language learning experience designed for modern families who want to actively participate in their children’s learning journey.

Instead of passive screen time or rigid tutoring systems, Learnbox creates playful, personalized language-learning moments families can enjoy together — wherever life happens: at home, while traveling, during weekends, on holidays, or while living remotely.

Every month, families receive curated learning boxes tailored to their children’s:

* age
* language level
* interests
* learning challenges
* educational goals
* lifestyle context

The experience combines learning, exploration, conversation, creativity, and family bonding into one adaptive subscription product.


## Vision

To make language learning feel like a shared family adventure instead of a school obligation.

Learnbox encourages:

* curiosity over perfection
* participation over performance
* confidence through play
* authentic communication
* joyful discovery of the world


## Core Value Proposition

Learnbox helps parents become active participants in their children’s language learning through personalized physical learning experiences.

## Local provider network and roots

Learnbox is intentionally **small, woman-led, and rooted in territory**. We partner with a **network of local providers** who share that conviction.

**What we showcase (including on the landing page in the MVP):**

* Our providers are **local companies** led by **women and non-binary people**
* They **employ people with different abilities**, reflecting a broad, inclusive idea of talent and care
* Together we are a **network of committed people** who believe education can **change the world**

**Why this matters for the value proposition:** Families are not buying an anonymous kit — they are connecting with **real people and real places**, aligned with Learnbox’s family-centered, emotionally grounded brand.

**Post-MVP (not in the first release):** Users will eventually be able to **browse provider catalogs** and **pick and choose from individual portfolios** (e.g. favor certain makers or specialties). That capability is **out of scope for the MVP** because of **logistics and operations complexity** (fulfillment, inventory splits, SLAs across many small suppliers). Until then, the product **communicates the network and its values** without offering per-provider shopping.
